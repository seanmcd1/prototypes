Why we are consulting

In July 2025 the Government published its response to an initial consultation on the proposed approach to siting fusion energy facilities. That response confirmed that the planning process for fusion energy facilities should be aligned with other complex generation facilities by developing a National Policy Statement.

The Government is now consulting on a draft National Policy Statement for Fusion Energy Generation (EN-8). This consultation focuses on the specific content of draft EN-8 and other documents published alongside it.

This consultation does not revisit earlier questions about whether a fusion-specific NPS is required, whether it should be technology-agnostic, or whether it should take an open-sited approach.

About the Fusion Energy Programme

The UK Government has identified the development of fusion energy as a strategic priority, backed by record investment of over £2.5 billion in fusion research and development.

Background

A National Policy Statement (NPS) is a statutory document designated under the Planning Act 2008 which sets out the Government's policy for decisions on applications for development consent for major infrastructure projects, known as nationally significant infrastructure projects (NSIPs).

The Overarching National Policy Statement for Energy (EN-1) sets out the Government's energy infrastructure policy, including the need case for new low-carbon energy infrastructure and the Critical National Priority afforded to such infrastructure.

UK Fusion Investment

£2.5 billion+ invested in fusion R&D, including £1.3 billion for the Spherical Tokamak for Energy Production (STEP) prototype fusion power plant.

What is draft EN-8?

Draft EN-8 sets out Government's policy for the assessment and determination of applications for development consent for nationally significant fusion energy infrastructure. Key features:

  • Technology agnostic — applies to all fusion approaches regardless of device design
  • Output agnostic — recognises that fusion infrastructure may generate thermal as well as electrical energy
  • Open sited — no specific sites are identified
  • No population density restrictions — reflecting the low radiological risk of fusion

Documents published for consultation

Draft EN-8

The main National Policy Statement for fusion energy generation — the core document for this consultation.

Appraisal of Sustainability

Assesses environmental, social and economic effects of implementing draft EN-8.

Habitats Regulations Assessment

Assesses whether implementation is likely to affect habitats sites.

Deep dive: 19 assessment considerations in draft EN-8

Draft EN-8 includes 19 fusion-specific assessment considerations grouped into four themes:

  • Environmental: Biodiversity, landscape, water, air quality, noise, waste, flood risk, historic environment, land use
  • Safety & Security: Radiological protection, hazardous substances, site security
  • Operational: Grid connection, transport, socio-economic impacts, community engagement
  • Developmental: Design quality, climate resilience, decommissioning

Consultation Questions

There are 5 questions across 5 parts. Your response will be most useful if it directly addresses these questions, though further comments are welcome.

The government will give greater weight to responses based on argument and evidence, rather than simple expressions of support or opposition.

1 Part A: Scope and clarity

Does draft EN-8, when read alongside EN-1 (and other relevant National Policy Statements), provide a clear, coherent and appropriate planning framework for fusion energy infrastructure?

Response options: Yes · Partially · No · Not sure

Please explain your answer, focusing on the overall clarity, coherence and appropriateness of the framework.

2 Part B: Role, scope and assessment principles

Do Sections 1 and 2 of draft EN-8 provide sufficient clarity on the role, scope and assessment principles of the fusion energy National Policy Statement?

Response options: Yes · Partially · No · Not sure

If 'No' or 'Partially', please explain what changes would be beneficial, referencing specific sections where possible.

3 Part C: Assessment considerations

Are the approaches set out for each consideration adequate and appropriate for all fusion energy infrastructure, making clear what, if anything, is required beyond the requirements of EN-1?

Response options: Yes · Partially · No · Not sure

If 'No' or 'Partially', please identify any considerations where the approach may be inadequate and suggest changes.

4 Part D: Environmental assessments

Are the environmental assessments (Appraisal of Sustainability and Habitats Regulations Assessment) for draft EN-8 reasonable and appropriate?

Response options: Yes · Partially · No · Not sure

If 'No' or 'Partially', please reference the specific section(s) in your response.

5 Part E: Further comments

Do you have any other comments on draft EN-8?

Response options: Yes · No

If 'Yes', please provide further comments, referencing specific section(s) where relevant.

Privacy notice

How we handle the information you provide in your consultation response.

Data controller

The Department for Energy Security and Net Zero (DESNZ) is the data controller for data collected during this consultation.

What data we collect

  • Your stakeholder type and organisation name (if provided)
  • Your responses to consultation questions
  • Any additional feedback or evidence you provide
  • Contact details (name, email, phone) — only if you choose to provide them
  • Submission timestamp and reference number

Legal basis

We process this data under Article 6(1)(e) of the UK GDPR — processing necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest or in the exercise of official authority.

How we use your data

  • To analyse consultation responses and inform policy decisions on draft EN-8
  • To produce a published summary of responses (without personal names or contact details)
  • If you provide contact details and consent, to follow up on your response

Disclosure

Information you provide may be disclosed in accordance with UK legislation (the Freedom of Information Act 2000, the Data Protection Act 2018, and the Environmental Information Regulations 2004).

!

If you want information you provide to be treated as confidential, please tell us — but be aware that we cannot guarantee confidentiality in all circumstances.

Data retention

Consultation responses will be retained for up to 3 years after the designation of EN-8, after which they will be securely destroyed.

Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to access, rectify, or erase your personal data. Contact us at fusionregulation@energysecurity.gov.uk.

Complaints

If you have concerns about how your data has been handled, you can complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.
